It's interest for technology began at "Instituto Maria Auxiliadora" High School, where he had the opportunity to deal with educational robotics extra-class activity, where professor Alexandre Amaral gave classes using LEGO Mindstorms kits to teach various topics from architecture assembly to programming introduction. After, the OBR (Brazilian Robotics Olympiad) was presented to put in practice the knowledge acquired. The mission is based on a firefighter robot that needs to follow a black line and to dodge possible obstacles on the way, at the end rescuing a victim represented by a soda can. Our team successfully got the first place on the state region, and 5th place on National level.

Fortunately, the author passed the national exam "ENEM" before finishing High School last year, ingressing on "Science and Technology" undergraduation course at the Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte. While participating on robotics competition before, the author met professor Aquiles which helped to organize the competition. From there, the author searched Aquiles to participate on Scientific initiation inside university, having opportunities to learn about educational robotics, web and app development, embedded systems, also to participate on various events involving robotics competitions, educational robotics lectures and arduino workshops.

The video shows the exposure of developed projects from the interdisciplinary group of mechatronics (GIM), which happened at CIENTEC. The circuits show by the author refer to basic concepts of common components such as LEDs potentiometers and sensors.

While working with professor Aquiles on the Lab of Automation and Robotics (LAR), the author took place on the ongoing project "One robot per student" (URA), supposed to deliver low cost robots to public schools, enhancing country education with educational robotics, as seen on the video:

The robot structure is built with cardboard to lower the final price, the embedded system consists of low cost motors and sensors, with an Arduino nano to control the system. The Bluetooth module communicates with a cellphone, so the user can control different modes of operation.

Also had the opportunity to work with professor Laurita , on the project called 10 dimensions, supposed to control a light station based on wind speed and direction to enhance teather shows.

Right after finishing undergraduation second year, the author applied to participate on Science without Borders gorvernment program being successfully selected to study on California State University, East Bay. There, he could improve his english language living with native english speakers, and experience different cultures from principal nationalities in the world, California is well know to aggregate people from various countries.

Not only learning about different cultures, but the exchange program was an excellent opportunity to acquire academic knowledge, taking different classes with syllicon valley professors who deals with state of art approaches. The author also participated on local events involving the studied undergraduation topics such as hackatons and meetup meetings. He likewise, integrated the CSUEB IEEE group, participating on technical tours and meetings.

The first contact with microeletronics came while taking professors Tandon VLSI class, by using open source tools such as Magic for layout and IRSIM for simulation. After the class finished, the author searched the professor to parcitipate on a university project, Tandon suggested the author to begin the study on FPGA architectures. From Fall until Spring of 2016 the basic concepts of FPGA architecture were developed using a mix of Mentor and IBM VLSI tools, at Summer of 2016 the Science without Borders allowed the students to apply for a internship or university research, the author chose to continue on with FPGA project.

Coming back to his home city, the author kept strong interest on digital microeletronics field. After, he had a conversation with professor Julio about the various topics the author have studied, and pointed professor Diomadson who has a strong background on microeletronics in general. Diomadson asked to keep the FPGA project going, who strongly complemented on the different project topics, including the introduction of Cadence VLSI tools and good paper writing practices.

The author finished Science and Tecnology undergraduation which consists of a 2 cycle course and decided to enter computer engineering for the second cycle. He hopes to keep meeting opportunities as life brought until now.
